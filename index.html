Here's an overview of our project.

The SOGIE Bill has encountered strong opposition due to the controversy surrounding its implementation in a predominantly Catholic country. In light of this, this research was sought out to examine if there is a prominent group responsible for propagating misinformation or disinformation regarding the provisions of the bill on Twitter.

Research Question

What is the dominant religion of the Twitter accounts who
vocally oppose the SOGIE Bill believing that it will legalize same sex marriage?

Hypothesis

Twitter accounts who proudly associated themselves with Christianity were most likely to oppose the SOGIE Bill and spread mis/disinformation about the bill’s provisions.

Null Hypothesis

Christian and non-Christian Twitter accounts were equally likely to oppose the SOGIE Bill and spread mis/disinformation about the bill’s provisions.

Action Plan

Analyze Twitter Accounts that posted mis/disinformation regarding the SOGIE Bill
and check their tweets and biography for religious keywords.

Data Exploration

We explore the data that we've collected to make sure they are aligned with the objectives of our study.

Data Collection

We mined the internet for fake news data. We obtained tweets manually and by using a Python script. Since we want to explore the tweets which spread mis/disinformation about the SOGIE Bill, these are the keywords that we used to collect tweets: "SOGIE Bill", "#NoToSOGIE", "#JunkSOGIEBill" Note that these keywords are not case sensitive and may be separated i.e. "sogie" and "bill" were also used as keywords.

Data Structure Overview

The group was able to collect 128 samples with 37 features from 81 unique Twitter accounts. Note that not all of these features might be relevant to the the study, and will be discussed later on. Moreover, some features have also been added to help analyze the data in the context of the study's research question.

Data Cleaning and Formatting

Out of the 37, the features with the highest percentage of null values are:

  • Remarks: 99.22%
  • Screenshot: 97.66%
  • URL Redirects to: 91.41%
  • Views: 81.25%
  • Christian Keyword: 53.13%

Since these features are not necessarily relevant to our hypothesis, we ignore these null values for now. We show some features and their type in the table below.

Data Visualization

Our study aims to compare the Christian and non-Christian tweets that have vocally opposed and have spread mis/disinformation about the SOGIE Bill in the Philippines. For this purpose, we manually tagged tweets and profiles as Christian and non-Christian based on the following rules. Note that Christian keywords were taken from this Glossary of Christian terms.

  • If the Twitter profile (bio, name, username) contains a Christian keyword.
  • If the tweet contains a Christian keyword.
  • If the profile has been previously tagged as "Christian."

We show the count of the Christian and non-Christian tweets in the chart below.

We observe that the number of tweets tagged as Christian is more than the non-Christian tweets. Exploring the numerical features of the Christian and non-Christian tweets, we can also compare the tweets' engagement and interaction.



In the table above, we can observe that Christian tweets have a higher mean value compared to the non-Christian tweets in terms of engagements. Which includes Likes, Retweets, Replies, and Quote Tweets. Even though non-Christian tweets have a higher mean value of Followers.

Moreover, we can also compare the account types of the Christian and non-Christian tweets. We show the distribution in the figure below.


We observe that there are more Anonymous account types in the dataset, and that for each account type, there are more Christian tweets.

Overall

Through our data exploration, we were able to show that:

  • There are more Christian tweets which opposed the SOGIE Bill by spreading mis/disinformation.
  • These tweets got more interaction.
  • The most common words found in tweets are right, bill, God, law, equality, and people.
